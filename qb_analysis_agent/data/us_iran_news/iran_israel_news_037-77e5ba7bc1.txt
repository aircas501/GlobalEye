US and Israeli Air Campaign
The IDF confirmed on March 14 that it killed two senior Iranian Khatam ol Anbia Central Headquarters intelligence officials, Abdollah Jalali Nassab and Amir Shariat, on March 13.[46] The IDF stated that Nassab and Shariat had replaced Khatam ol Anbia Central Headquarters Intelligence Chief Saleh Asadi, whom Israel targeted and killed in airstrikes on February 28.[47] The Khatam ol Anbia Central Headquarters oversees and coordinates Iranian military activities, serving as the main authority for operational command and control.[48]
The United States struck Iranian military infrastructure on Kharg Island in Bushehr Province on March 13, in part to degrade Iran's ability to threaten shipping in the Strait of Hormuz. US Central Command (CENTCOM) stated that US forces conducted large-scale precision strikes targeting Kharg Island, destroying naval mine storage facilities, missile storage facilities, and other military sites.[49] CENTCOM added that it struck over 90 Iranian military targets on the island.[50] Several open-source intelligence (OSINT) accounts, including "Stinky," "Pixel," "Wooltiger Hunter," and Mitchell Ulrich, geolocated CENTCOM footage that shows that the United States struck Kharg Airport, the Kharg Air Defense Complex, and a Matla ul Fajr radar on Kharg Island.[51] The Matla ul Fajr radar reportedly has long-range early warning and air surveillance capabilities with a detection range of up to 500 kilometers for identifying and tracking aerial targets.
